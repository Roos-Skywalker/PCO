Personal SWOT analysis

What are your strengths?

1. My honesty. I will always offer honest and constructive feedback. 2. Work ethic. Whenever possible, I will stay at school until I have finished my assignment, unable to get the help needed to progress, or I have exhausted all energy. 3. I enjoy figuring out new things whenever I am not under (too much) pressure. 4. It brings me joy to see my creations come closer and closer to completion. 5. I am very sensitive to details and good coding practices. I like making everything look organised and nice. 6. I take joy and pride in achieving high marks. 7. I no longer seek social approval to do what I need or want to do. Nor do I crave validation anymore.


What are your weaknesses?

1. I am easily distracted, but I am taking medication to help with my ADD. 2. My self-esteem is tied to my performance. 3. I heavily rely on body language to interpret intention. 4. My honesty can cause issues working with a shy or prideful team. I can often recognise social issues, but I lack the intuition natural to neurotypical people to understand why they feel the way they do. To avoid conflicts I take on an apologetic tone before or right after engagement. 5. I cannot keep up physically with other students on fun trips.


Which opportunities do you see?


Which threats do you see?

1. As the studies advance I will have less and less direct access to teachers. I see myself asking a lot of questions and getting stuck often with new languages. 2. I have been rated by the UWV (government organisation that manages welfare and unemployment benefits) to be fit for 34 hours of work a week. The study is rated at 40 hours. Through sheer stubbornness and will shall I force myself to overextend, but I would not be surprised if I would need to redo a year. 3. I have dyscalculia, which means I have difficulties recognising and applying numbers in the right context, as well as having a distorted sense of time.
