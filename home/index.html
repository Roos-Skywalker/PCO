Okay, but why?

Six years ago I enrolled as well. I passed my propaedeutic year, but due to personal circumstances and COVID-19 I was unable to continue.

Now unfettered by fear of failure and resource restraints, I am able to pursue college once more.

Six year gap, what happened?

Between those years I have worked various jobs; chiefly at Saman Group (a company that specialises in installing green energy solutions and home heating).

I served three roles: customer service data processing and automation; financial administration and automation; and finally Service & Maintenance where I created and maintained customer contracts.

Unfortunately, after solar panel market crash there was insufficient work available to me, as well as the IT team working on an AI-based solution to replace me, after which my contract wasn't converted into a permanent one.

After this encounter I decided I would return to school if I could not secure a permanent contract next job.

A loser catching bigger losers

That next job was working for the police as an assistant detective. My job was to investigate cybercrime and digital forensics.

I recognised suspects on camera, and searched seized devices such as phones and laptops for incriminating evidence.

Unfortunately, my mentor was unable to sufficiently assist me due to his cancer treatments. Due to understaffing they could not find a suitable replacement, so I was let go.

That's when I had enough. It was time to go back to my roots; my passion.
